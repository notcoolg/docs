# Channels

Channels are a broadcast-style communication format in eChat. Unlike group chats where everyone can send messages freely, channels are designed for **one-to-many communication**, perfect for announcements, community updates, newsletters, and public figures.

## Groups vs. Channels

| Feature | Group Chat | Channel |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Who can post | All members | Admins & Owner only |
| Best for | Team collaboration, conversation | Announcements, broadcasts |
| Member interaction | Bi-directional | Subscribers receive messages |
| Typical use case | Friends, teams, projects | Communities, brands, news |

## Creating a channel

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Chat screen">
    Go to the main **Chat** screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the new conversation menu">
    Tap the **\+** or **compose** icon and select **New Channel**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name your channel">
    Enter a name for the channel — for example, _"Company Updates"_, _"Gaming Community"_, or _"Photography Tips"_.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add initial members">
    Add the first members (subscribers) by searching for contacts and selecting them. You can add more later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the channel">
    Tap **Create** to finalize. The channel is immediately available in your chat list.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Posting to a channel

As a channel **Owner** or **Admin**, you can post messages, images, videos, GIFs, and voice notes just like in any other conversation.

Members of the channel will receive these messages but cannot reply to the channel directly.

## Managing channel members

To add or manage members:

1. Tap the **channel name** or **info icon** at the top of the channel.
2. In the **Conversation Info** panel, you can:
   - View the full member list
   - Add new members (Admins and Owner only)
   - Remove members (Admins and Owner only)
   - Promote members to Admin

## Renaming a channel

1. Open the **Conversation Info** panel.
2. Tap the channel name at the top.
3. Edit the name and confirm.

Only **Admins** and the **Owner** can rename the channel.

## Leaving a channel

To leave a channel you're subscribed to:

1. Open the **Conversation Info** panel.
2. Tap **Leave Channel** at the bottom.

You'll stop receiving messages from that channel.

## Channel Network

eChat has a feature called **Channel Network**, which allow you to find and join any verified/public channel on the entire platform.

## Tips for running a great channel

- **Post consistently** — Subscribers are more likely to stay engaged if you post regularly.
- **Use rich media** — Images, videos, and GIFs make posts more eye-catching than text alone.
- **Keep posts focused** — Channels work best when they have a clear topic or purpose.
- **Announce your channel** — Share your Chattercode and the channel name with potential subscribers.

---

<Tip>
  If you want community interaction alongside your broadcast content, consider running both a channel (for announcements) and a group (for discussion) on the same topic.
</Tip>
