# Appearance

eChat is designed to look great no matter your preference. You can switch between **dark mode** and **light mode** at any time — and your choice syncs across your sessions.

## Switching between dark and light mode

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    Tap the **Settings** icon (⚙️) in the navigation menu or sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the Theme option">
    In the Settings screen, locate the **Theme** or **Appearance** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Toggle the mode">
    Tap the toggle to switch between **Dark Mode** and **Light Mode**. The change applies immediately — no need to restart.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Dark mode

eChat's dark mode uses deep, near-black backgrounds (`#151520`) with light text and subtle accent colors. It's easy on the eyes in low-light environments and reduces eye strain during evening or nighttime use.

**Good for:**

- Using eChat at night or in a dark room
- Reducing eye fatigue in low light
- Saving battery on OLED displays

## Light mode

Light mode uses bright, clean backgrounds with dark text — ideal for use in well-lit environments or when you prefer a traditional UI.

**Good for:**

- Daytime use or well-lit rooms
- Reading long messages more easily
- Matching system-wide light mode preferences

## Design language

eChat uses the **MateFluency** design system, developed by EcoWestern. This system brings:

- Smooth, rounded UI elements (16px border radius)
- Naturalism effects in the call interface
- Consistent spacing and typography across the app
- **Google Sans Flex** typefaces for clean, readable text
- Thoughtful color tokens that adapt between dark and light modes

The result is a modern, polished interface that feels native on any device.

## Font sizes and accessibility

eChat respects your system's font size settings on mobile and desktop. If you've increased your system's default font size for accessibility, eChat's text will scale accordingly.

---

<Tip>
  If you're unsure which mode to use, try **dark mode** — most eChat users prefer it and the app was designed with dark mode as the primary experience.
</Tip>
