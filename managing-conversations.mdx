---
title: "Managing Conversations"
---

Azura stores all of your conversations so you can return to any chat at any time. This guide explains how to navigate conversation history, organize chats, and keep things tidy.

## The Sidebar

The sidebar on the left of the screen is your conversation hub. It shows a list of all your past and current conversations, most recent at the top.

- **Click a conversation** to open it and continue where you left off
- **The active conversation** is highlighted in the list
- All conversation history, including messages, artifacts, and tool results, is preserved exactly as you left it

---

## Starting a New Conversation

To start a fresh chat:

- Click the **New Chat** button or the `+` icon in the header/sidebar
- The chat area will clear and you'll see the Azura welcome screen
- Your previous conversations remain in the sidebar

> **Tip:** Starting a new conversation is a good way to reset context when moving to a completely different topic.

---

## Clearing the Current Conversation

If you want to wipe the current conversation and start fresh (without creating a new chat entry):

- Type `/clear` in the Omnibox and press Enter

This clears the message history in the current session.

---

## Summarizing a Long Conversation

When a conversation has gone on for a while and you want a concise recap:

- Type `/summarize` in the Omnibox and press Enter

Azura will read through the conversation and write a summary of the key points, decisions, and any outstanding items.

This is especially useful before starting a new phase of a project or sharing context with someone else.

---

## Token Usage and Context Limits

Each conversation has a **context window**, the maximum amount of text Azura can reference at once. As a conversation grows, you get closer to this limit.

To check how many tokens you're using:

```text
/tokens
```

Azura will display your current input and output token counts for the session.

**What to do when you're near the limit:**

1. **Summarize** — use `/summarize` to ask Azura to compress the conversation into a shorter summary, then continue from there
2. **Start fresh** — begin a new conversation. You can paste in a short summary from the previous chat to re-establish context
3. **Switch models** — different models have different context window sizes

---

## Returning to a Past Conversation

Your full conversation history is saved in the sidebar. Simply click any conversation in the list to re-open it.

You can:

- Pick up exactly where you left off
- View all messages, tool results, and artifacts
- Continue chatting — the conversation history is sent as context to Azura

---

## Artifacts and Conversations

Each conversation has its own set of artifacts stored in the artifact panel. When you return to a conversation, the artifacts from that session are loaded automatically.

Use `/artifacts` to toggle the panel open if it's not already visible.

---

## Tips for Power Users

- **Name your conversations meaningfully** —  Give conversations descriptive names so you can find them quickly later.
- **Start new conversations for new topics** — this keeps context clean and prevents Azura from mixing up information between unrelated projects.
- **Use `/summarize` before hitting the token limit** — don't wait until you can't send more messages. Summarize early to preserve the flow of the conversation.
- **Return to old conversations for reference** — your full chat history is an easy way to look up something Azura explained before without re-asking.
