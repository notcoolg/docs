# Voice Notes

Sometimes it's faster (and more expressive) to speak than to type. eChat lets you record and send **voice notes** directly in any conversation. Recipients can play them back with a built-in waveform player that shows the audio shape and lets you scrub through the recording.

## Recording a voice note

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a conversation">
    Navigate to the conversation where you want to send a voice note.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap and hold the microphone icon">
    In the message input bar, **press and hold** the **microphone** icon (🎤). Recording starts immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Speak your message">
    Speak clearly into your device's microphone. The recording is live the moment you press and hold.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Release to send">
    **Let go** of the microphone icon to stop recording and automatically send the voice note. The audio is uploaded and sent as a message.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  If you release and don't want to send, swipe left while holding the microphone icon to **cancel** the recording before it sends.
</Warning>

## Playing back a voice note

When you receive a voice note, it appears as an audio player in the conversation:

1. Tap the **play button** (▶) to start playback.
2. The **waveform** shows the shape of the audio so you can see quieter and louder parts at a glance.
3. Tap and drag along the waveform to **scrub** to any point in the recording.
4. Tap the **pause button** (⏸) to pause playback.

## Tips for great voice notes

- **Find a quiet spot** — Background noise is picked up by your microphone. A quieter environment makes your voice notes easier to understand.
- **Keep it concise** — Voice notes work best for short messages (a few seconds to a minute). For longer topics, consider breaking them into multiple recordings.
- **Speak close to the mic** — Hold your phone normally to your face or keep a short distance from your laptop/desktop microphone.

## Microphone permissions

eChat needs access to your microphone to record voice notes. If you haven't granted this permission yet:

- **On mobile**: When you first try to record, your browser or OS will prompt you to allow microphone access. Tap **Allow**.
- **On desktop**: Your browser will show a permission bar at the top. Click **Allow**.

If you previously denied microphone access:
1. Tap the **lock icon** or **settings icon** in your browser's address bar.
2. Find **Microphone** and change it to **Allow**.
3. Refresh the page if needed.
