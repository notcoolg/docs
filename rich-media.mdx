# Sharing Photos & Videos

eChat lets you share photos and videos directly in any conversation. Files are uploaded securely and can be viewed right inside the chat.

## Sending an image or video

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a conversation">
    Tap the conversation where you want to share media.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap the attachment icon">
    In the message input bar, tap the **paperclip / attachment** icon to open the attachment picker.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your file">
    Select **Photo** or **Video** from the menu, then choose the file from your device's gallery or file system.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send">
    The file will upload automatically. Once ready, it's sent to the conversation and displayed inline.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Viewing shared media

- **Images** appear as thumbnails directly in the message stream. Tap any image to open it in the **full-screen viewer**.
- **Videos** display with a play button. Tap to play the video inline.
- In the full-screen viewer, you can pinch to zoom, swipe to browse other shared images, and close by tapping the **✕** button.

## Supported file types

| Type | Supported formats |
|---|---|
| Images | JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF (animated) |
| Videos | MP4, WebM, MOV |

<Note>
  Very large files may take a moment to upload depending on your connection speed. A progress indicator will appear while the upload is in progress.
</Note>

## Tips for media sharing

- **Photos**: For the best quality, share images as JPEG or PNG. Screenshots work great too.
- **Videos**: Keep videos short (under a minute) for faster uploads and smoother playback.
- **Storage**: Shared files are stored securely on eChat's servers and are accessible to all conversation participants.

## Downloading media

To save a photo or video to your device:
1. Open the media in the full-screen viewer.
2. On mobile, press and hold the image/video and select **Save to Photos** or **Download**.
3. On desktop, right-click and choose **Save image as…** or **Save video as…**.

---

<Tip>
  You can send multiple images in a row by sending one after another — they'll group together nicely in the conversation feed.
</Tip>
