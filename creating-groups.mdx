# Creating Groups

Group chats let you have a shared conversation with multiple people at once. Whether it's a friend group, a project team, or a family chat, groups are the perfect way to keep everyone on the same page.

## What is a group?

A **group** is a multi-participant conversation where every member can send and receive messages. Groups have:

- A **group name** (you can change it later)
- An optional **group photo** or avatar
- **Member roles**: Owner, Admin, and Member
- The ability to **add or remove members** over time

## How to create a group

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Chat screen">
    Navigate to the main **Chat** screen in eChat.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap the new chat / compose icon">
    Tap the **\+** or **compose** icon, then select **New Group** from the options.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name your group">
    Enter a name for your group — something descriptive like "Family", "Project Team", or "Weekend Plans".
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add participants">
    Search for contacts by name and add the people you want in the group. You can add multiple people at once.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the group">
    Tap **Create** (or the equivalent confirm button). The group conversation is created immediately and all members are added.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Group size

Groups support multiple participants. You can add or remove members at any time after creation.

## Who gets added first?

When you create a group, you are automatically the **Owner** with full admin privileges. All other members are added as **Members** by default. You can promote members to **Admin** later.

## After creating a group

Once the group is created:

- It appears in your **Chat** list.
- All members receive a notification that they've been added.
- You can immediately start messaging.

## Groups vs. Channels

| Feature | Group | Channel |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Who can send messages | All members | Admins/owners only |
| Best for | Conversations, team chat | Announcements, broadcasts |
| Typical size | Small to medium | Any size |
| Member roles | Owner, Admin, Member | Owner, Admin, Member |

If you want to broadcast messages to a large audience without everyone replying, consider creating a **Channel** instead. See [Channels](/channels).
