# Call Settings

eChat gives you control over your audio and video configuration during calls, so you can tailor the experience to your setup and environment.

## Accessing call settings

You can adjust call settings **during an active call**:

1. On the call screen, tap the **settings icon** (⚙️) in the control bar.
2. The call settings panel will open as an overlay.

You can also configure some settings **before accepting a call** in the incoming call screen.

## Audio settings

### Echo cancellation
Prevents your speaker output from being picked up by your microphone and sent back to the other person as an echo.

- **Recommended**: Keep this **on** unless you're using professional studio headphones/monitors.

### Noise suppression
Filters out ambient background sounds — fan noise, keyboard clicks, TV in the background, etc.

- **Recommended**: Keep this **on** for most environments.

### Auto gain control
Automatically adjusts your microphone sensitivity so your voice stays at a consistent volume even as you move.

- **Recommended**: Keep this **on** unless you're using a dedicated microphone with its own gain control.

## Video settings

### Camera on/off
Toggle your camera before or during the call using the **camera icon** in the control bar.

- Turn camera **off** if you need a break from being on screen, or if you have a poor internet connection (no camera = less bandwidth used).

### Choosing audio/video devices

If you have multiple microphones, speakers, or cameras connected to your device, eChat will use your system default. To change the active device:
- Open **Call Settings** during the call.
- Use the dropdown menus to select your preferred microphone and speaker.

<Note>
  Device selection options are available on desktop browsers. On mobile, eChat uses the device's front/rear camera and built-in microphone by default.
</Note>

## Pre-call settings

When you receive an incoming call, you can:
- Toggle your **microphone** on or off before answering.
- Toggle your **camera** on or off before answering.

This gives you a moment to prepare before the other person sees or hears you.

## Troubleshooting call quality

| Issue | Try this |
|---|---|
| Echo on the call | Enable echo cancellation in Call Settings |
| Loud background noise | Enable noise suppression in Call Settings |
| Low or inconsistent voice volume | Enable auto gain control |
| Choppy audio or video | Switch to Wi-Fi, or turn off your camera to save bandwidth |
| Can't be heard | Check that your browser/OS has microphone permission for eChat |
| Can't hear others | Check your device's speaker/headphone volume and system audio settings |
| Camera not working | Check that your browser/OS has camera permission for eChat |

## Microphone and camera permissions

eChat needs permission to access your microphone and camera for calls. If you're having trouble:

1. Click the **lock icon** (🔒) or **info icon** (ℹ) in your browser's address bar.
2. Set **Microphone** to **Allow**.
3. Set **Camera** to **Allow** (for video calls).
4. Reload the page if prompted.

---

<Tip>
  If you're in a noisy environment and can't use headphones, try keeping noise suppression on and muting yourself when you're not actively speaking — this prevents your background noise from disrupting the call for everyone.
</Tip>
