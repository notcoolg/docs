# Tips & Tricks

Whether you're just getting started or you've been using eChat for a while, these tips will help you communicate faster, smarter, and more expressively.

---

## Messaging tips

### Use Markdown for richer messages

eChat renders Markdown in messages. Here are the most useful formatting shortcuts:

| What you type | What they see |
|---|---|
| `**hello**` | **hello** (bold) |
| `*hello*` | *hello* (italic) |
| `` `code` `` | `code` (inline code) |
| `- item` | • item (bullet list) |
| `**note:** text` | **note:** text (bold label) |

Use bold to highlight key points, italic for emphasis, and code formatting when sharing technical content.

### Shift + Enter for multi-line messages

Pressing **Enter** sends your message. Pressing **Shift + Enter** inserts a line break instead, letting you write multi-paragraph messages before sending.

### Reply to keep threads clear

In busy group chats, always use the **Reply** feature when responding to a specific message. It shows the quoted message alongside your reply so everyone knows what you're referring to — no more "what are you replying to?" confusion.

### React instead of typing "ok"

For simple acknowledgments, reactions are faster and cleaner than typing a message. A quick 👍 says everything without cluttering the conversation.

### Edit your messages

If you send a message with a typo or want to clarify something, **long-press** the message and tap **Edit**. The corrected message shows an *(edited)* tag, so recipients know it was updated.

## GIF & media tips

### Search GIFs by emotion, not object

The best GIF searches are emotion-based: try *"excited"*, *"not sure"*, *"slow clap"*, *"awkward"*, or *"mind blown"* for great results. Searching for objects usually returns less expressive results.

### Send voice notes when typing is tedious

Voice notes are perfect when you have a long or nuanced message to send. They're faster to record than type, and your tone of voice adds context that text can't convey. Great for emotional support, complex explanations, or a casual chat.

### Group images together

Sending photos one after another in quick succession causes them to display as a natural photo burst in the conversation. It's a clean way to share a small album without creating clutter.

## Call tips

### Mute yourself when you're not speaking

In any call — especially group calls — mute yourself when you're not actively talking. This prevents background noise (fans, pets, keyboard) from disrupting the call for others, and improves overall audio quality.

### Use headphones or earbuds

Headphones prevent your speaker audio from leaking into your microphone (echo). Even basic earbuds make a big difference in call quality for everyone.

### Turn off video on a weak connection

If your connection is unreliable, turning off your camera frees up bandwidth for the audio stream. You'll have a clearer call at the cost of video.

### Pre-configure audio before answering

When an incoming call arrives, use the **microphone and camera toggles** on the incoming call screen to set your audio/video preferences *before* you answer. No awkward scrambling after you pick up.

### Enable noise suppression in a noisy place

If you're in a coffee shop, on transit, or anywhere noisy, open **Call Settings** and make sure **noise suppression** is turned on. It filters out ambient noise so your voice comes through clearly.

## App & setup tips

### Install eChat for the best experience

The installed **PWA** version of eChat is faster, more reliable, and supports full push notifications (including incoming call alerts even when your browser is closed). See [Installing eChat](/getting-started/installing-the-app) for platform-specific instructions.

### Use conversation tabs to filter your chats

On the Chat screen, use the **All / Channels / DMs** tabs to filter your conversations. If you follow several channels, switch to the **DMs** tab to focus on personal conversations without the noise.

### Share your Chattercode to grow your contact list

Your **Chattercode** is your public identity on eChat. Drop it in a bio, share it in other social apps, or text it to friends to make it easy for them to add you without needing your email.

### Pin or star important conversations

Keep your most-used conversations easily accessible. Use the conversation management options to pin or prioritize chats so they're always at the top of your list.

### Keep eChat updated

eChat updates in the background, but when you see the **"Update available"** banner, tap **Reload** to get the latest features and improvements right away.

## Privacy & etiquette tips

### Chattercode ≠ phone number

Your Chattercode is yours to share or keep private. Unlike a phone number, sharing it doesn't expose your personal contact information — it only lets people find you on eChat.

### Use channels for one-way announcements

If you run a community or want to share updates without managing replies, create a **channel** instead of a group. Only admins can post, keeping the feed clean and focused.

### Block proactively

Don't hesitate to use the **Block** feature. Blocking someone is private (they won't be notified) and takes effect immediately.
