# Group Calls

eChat supports **group audio and video calls** so you can connect with multiple people at once — whether it's a friend group hangout, a family catch-up, or a team standup.

## Starting a group call

Group calls are started from **group conversations**:

1. Open a **group chat**.
2. Tap the **phone icon** (audio) or **video icon** (video) in the top-right corner of the conversation.
3. All members of the group will receive an incoming call notification and can join.

## Joining a group call in progress

If a group call is already happening in one of your conversations, you'll see an **active call banner** in the group chat. Tap it to join the call mid-session.

<Note>
  You can join or leave a group call at any time — you don't have to be there from the start.
</Note>

## The group call screen

The group call interface is designed to show everyone clearly:

- Each participant has their own **video tile** (or avatar if camera is off).
- Tiles are arranged in a responsive grid that adjusts as people join or leave.
- **Audio waveforms** appear under active participants so you can see who's talking even when video is off.
- Your own video tile is shown so you can see what others see.

## Group call controls

| Control | Action |
|---|---|
| **Microphone icon** | Mute / unmute yourself |
| **Camera icon** | Turn your camera on / off |
| **End call button** | Leave the call (others continue) |
| **Settings icon** | Open audio/video device settings |

## Participant list

During a group call, you can see a list of all connected participants:
- Active speakers are highlighted.
- Muted participants show a muted mic indicator.
- Each participant's display name and avatar are shown.

## Leaving a group call

Tap the **red end call button** to leave. Unlike a 1-on-1 call, this only disconnects *you* — the group call continues for everyone else.

## Tips for smooth group calls

- **Mute yourself when not talking** — This reduces background noise for everyone else and improves call quality overall.
- **Use headphones** — Prevents echo and helps you hear everyone clearly.
- **Stable connection matters** — A wired or strong Wi-Fi connection gives the smoothest video. If your connection is weak, turning off your camera can improve the experience for you and others.
- **Find good lighting** — If you're on video, face a window or light source so others can see you clearly.
- **Keep your group call link accessible** — Pin the group conversation so it's easy to get back to quickly.

---

<Tip>
  In large group calls, it helps to take turns speaking rather than talking over each other. Use the mute button generously — mute yourself when listening and unmute to speak.
</Tip>
